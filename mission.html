Our Mission

Our Mission is to help equip women and their children so that they can be an active member of society. We are dedicated to helping them reform their lives to become the people God calls them to be. We are a lighthouse and a beacon of hope to all women and women with children who have a strong desire to change their way of life by implementing Biblical principles so that the cycle of chronic homelessness shall be broken.
We house families and individuals who have experienced crisis in their lives such as; loss of employment, housing or other life-controlling problems that rendered them homeless. Each potential resident must have a strong desire to change and develop a new way of life. We help our residents in many areas of achieving success. We assist them in finding gainful employment with local established companies, financial recovery classes, and much more. It is our goal to prepare families and individuals to become financially strong, emotionally balanced and self efficient in every way.
We prepare individuals who did not finish high school to take the high school Equivalence test to become more productive citizens in our local community with higher paying jobs.

What we do...

The Dream Center of Jackson has been transforming lives of the chronically homeless through the love of Jesus Christ for 15 years through its residential emergency ministry for women and their children in West Tennessee with the mission of “Rebuilding Lives, Restoring Hope, Renewing Dreams.” Our goal is to transform the chronically homeless into self-sufficient, spiritually, physically, and emotionally balanced members of society. This impacts our community making it a better place to live, work and play.

Why we do it...

Our goal is to transform the chronically homeless into self-sufficient, spiritually, physically, and emotionally balanced members of society. This impacts our community making it a better place to live, work and play. A Dream Center success story is a strong and educated Christian woman that has learned the skills and abilities necessary to escape dependency, abuse and addiction and provide for herself and her family.

How we do it...

There are numerous factors in the successful formula that has proven over the past 15 years to serve and treat these Tennesseans. First, the woman must be ready for a complete life change, be willing to do the necessary work, admit that her “old ways” are not working and agree to participate in proven treatment and recovery methods that are designed for her personal struggles. The first required class is our Biblically based “180” class which teaches how to turn away from old destructive habits. Taking time to revisit past hurts, traumas, mistakes, and regrets allows our women to turn away from her “old life” which lead to spiritual poverty, where she was consistently in unhealthy relationships, making poor decisions for herself and her family. The new, abundant life starts with the choice to turn her life 180 degrees and go in the direction of spiritual prosperity where God’s love abounds.

Learn About Our History Learn About Our Programs

On May 4, 2003, an F4 tornado struck the Jackson area. Due to this event, many families and individuals were left without a home. We obtained an old hospital that had been mostly vacant for several years. Now the victims of this tornado had a temporary place to stay. We handed out food and supplies daily in the devastated Jackson neighborhoods. During this period we housed up to 60 individuals and families nightly. They stayed with us until they found permanent housing or had their own homes renovated or rebuilt. In the 14 years since we recognize there is still a need and we are continuing to fill that need. We are a faith-based educational ministry for women and women with children. We rebuild lives, restore hope, and renew dreams. The women we serve have experienced crisis and/or loss in their lives. We provide all of the basic needs for our women. We assist with job readiness, resume building, finding gainful employment, and any other service that will prepare the women to achieve success. All classes are mandatory. Finance, nutrition, parenting, and Bible study are just some of the classes we offer. We have a mandate by God to help, support, love, and minister to these women through their tough times by instilling hope and applying Christian principles.

We provide several classes for our residents to help them reform and improve their lives

  • Class 180: this is our first required class that is a Biblically based “180” class which teaches how to turn away from old destructive habits.
  • Bible Classes
  • High School Equivalency Test Preparation
  • Finance and Budgeting
  • Job Readiness
  • Parenting
  • Cooking Nutritiously on a Budget
  • Computer 101
  • Sewing
  • Art
